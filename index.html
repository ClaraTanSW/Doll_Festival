Hinamatsuri - Doll Festival

March 3 marks the observance of the hinamatsuri (doll festival), one of five sekku, or seasonal festivals, celebrated through the year.

Katsuura Big Hina Matsuri at Tomisaki Shrine, Chiba Prefecture

One of the most interesting and unique displays of Hina Matsuri Dolls is at Tomisaki Shrine in Katsuura, a small fishing village in Chiba Prefecture near Tokyo.


Konosu Bikkuri Hina Matsuri Doll Festival

A spectacular seven meter (22.9 ft) high pyramid of traditional hina dolls can be seen at the shopping mall in front of JR Konosu Station in Saitama Prefecture near Tokyo.


Chirashi (Rice)

Layered chirashi sushi, which resembles a cake, is often enjoyed on Japanese Girl's Day

DOLL FESTIVAL

Hinamatsuri (雛祭り Hina-matsuri), also called Doll's Day or Girls' Day, is a special day in Japan. Hinamatsuri is celebrated each year on March 3rd. Platforms covered with a red carpet are used to display a set of ornamental dolls (雛人形 hina-ningyō) representing the Emperor, Empress, attendants, and musicians in traditional court dress of the Heian period. Doll's Festival—is celebrated on March 3 to pray for the health and happiness of young girls in Japan. Hinamatsuri, the name of the celebration in Japan, is marked by families displaying a set of hina dolls in the house and serving special food delicacies that are ceremonially beautiful and delicious.
